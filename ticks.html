Ticks

Ticks that have not been feeding from your cat are small, have eight legs, and vary from tan, red, black, or brown. Ticks that have been feeding from you cat will fill up with blood, become larger and can be mistaken for a lump or swelling.

  • Make sure your cat has a clean environment.
  • Your vet can advise about preventive medications.
  • If you get medication from a store, make sure it is for cats as it is unsafe to use dog medication on cats.
  • Combing your cat with a specific comb that contains chemicals will help remove ticks or fleas.
  • Using a vet-recommended collar that repels ticks and fleas is a good way of controlling the parasite.
